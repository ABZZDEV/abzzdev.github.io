GRAPHIC DESIGN PROCESS
Introduction:

When designing any material that is intended to be used in a professional capacity there are several important considerations that need to be accounted for. On top of this there are also several documents that can be produced in order to ensure that every element of a design is thoroughly examined and is incorporated into the final product. In order to ensure all requirments are met and the design process is smooth I maintain a pre-determined and flexible design process.


The Client:

As simple and obvious as it is, the Client is the first consideration that must be documented in my design process.


The Client’s Requirements:

This covers what the Client would like to be produced (even if the client is myself) and any details about the design that are critical.


The Target Audience:

When designing anything, the client generally isn't the only person who will be interacting with the content. In order to appeal to the target audience of your design you need to consider their needs too.


Legal and Ethical Considerations:

This consists of the measures that are taken by myself to ensure that legaly protected material (for example copyrighted or trademarked material) isn't infringed and ensuring that all terms and contractal obligations from my client are accounted for.


Success Criteria:

My success criteria is a list of things I aim for my content to achieve. I use this as a point of reference when reflecting on the design direction of my content.


Hardware Requirements:

I consider my hardware requirements to ensure I am able to produce the final product without being limited by my hardware. I also consider my hardware requirements to ensure I am not overly abitious with the scale of the digital media I create in order to maintain compatibility with the target audience's hardware.


Software Requirements:

This ties in with my previous point of ensuring I have all the suitable software ready for use before beginning a project in order to avoid unexpected issues or delays.


Timeline:

It is important for both myself and my clients to have a reliable timeline to refer over the duration of the creation of a project.


Target File-Formats, Types and Sizes:

File formats and sizes are also an important aspect of digital graphic design hence my additon of it to my design documentation. The file format determines factors such as file size and compatibility which can impact the end user experience heavily.


Asset List:

I make use of asset lists in my projects to ensure all items created are accounted for and all third party material is appropriately marked and referenced.


Moodboard:

The creation of a moodboard is one of the most important parts of my design process as it allows me to create new ideas while not straying too far from the original concept. The moodboard can also be used as a point of reference for me to maintain creativity throughout the project.


Storyboard:

I use storyboards to plan all of the major stages of an animation to make sure it nothing gets missed out and to ensure that it is logical and is realistic.


Technical Information:

This consists of all information of any 3D models, 2D sprite or animated materials. For example the size, weight and speed of an object. This allows me to animate and design in a way that accounts for the physics that an object would have. This also helps with instances where the object may have to be implemented into a virual world.
